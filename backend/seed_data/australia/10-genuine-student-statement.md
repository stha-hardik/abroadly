# Genuine Student Requirement and SOP for Australia

Use "Genuine Student" or "GS", not old "GTE", for current Student visa answers. Home Affairs replaced the old Genuine Temporary Entrant framing with the Genuine Student requirement. Some older training documents, provider forms, and student wording may still say GTE; translate that into GS unless quoting a provider's old form name.

## What Home Affairs Currently Asks

As of 29 May 2026, Home Affairs says the online Student visa application asks for responses in English to a set of Genuine Student questions. Each question has a 150-word limit. There is an additional question if the applicant has previously held a Student visa or is lodging in Australia from a non-student visa. Supporting documents should be attached in ImmiAccount.

## What a Strong GS/SOP Explains

- Why this exact course.
- Why this provider and campus.
- How the course connects to previous study and work.
- Why Australia is a logical destination compared with Nepal and other countries.
- How the student and sponsors can afford tuition, living costs, travel, OSHC, and family costs.
- Career plan after study, including realistic roles and how the Australian qualification helps.
- Explanation of gaps, backlogs, low grades, course changes, or visa history.
- Family, financial, social, and professional ties, without making unrealistic promises.

## Weak Answers

- "Australia is my dream country" without course logic.
- Copy-paste provider marketing.
- Choosing a cheaper unrelated course only for visa chances.
- Saying part-time work will pay tuition.
- Hiding spouse, children, refusal history, or previous study.
- Claiming guaranteed PR.
- Overly generic answers that could apply to any country/course.

## Suggested Answer Structure

For a student's GS draft, use this structure:

1. Profile: qualification, grades, English, work, and gap.
2. Course logic: why this field and why this level.
3. Provider logic: campus, curriculum, accreditation, fees, intake, facilities, support.
4. Australia logic: CRICOS-regulated international education, practical learning, course availability.
5. Finance: sponsor source, relationship, evidence, affordability without illegal work dependence.
6. Outcome: realistic career roles and next steps after graduation.
7. Risk explanations: gaps/backlogs/refusal/course change if applicable.

## Follow-Up Questions for GS Help

- What exact GS question are you answering?
- What is the 150-word limit for that question?
- What is your previous study and result?
- Why did you choose this provider over two alternatives?
- Who is funding you and what documents prove it?
- Do you have any refusal, gap, course change, or dependent family member?
