# Nepal NOC/NOL and Document Preparation for Australia

Nepali students normally need a No Objection Certificate/No Objection Letter from Nepal's Ministry of Education, Science and Technology before sending tuition money abroad through a Nepali bank. The official portal is https://noc.moest.gov.np/.

## When to Prepare NOC

Prepare NOC after receiving an offer letter and before paying tuition through a Nepali bank. The local Australia process sheet placed NOC/NOL after payment approval and before institutional payment. In practice, banks usually ask for NOC when processing foreign education payments.

## Common NOC Documents from the Training Checklist

- Original highest qualification transcript and copy.
- Citizenship certificate and copy.
- Offer letter/admission letter from the Australian education provider.
- Passport may be requested by current portal/process.
- Additional documents may be required depending on level, course, country, and current MoEST rules.

If the student changed provider, country, course, or level, ask whether the NOC details still match the actual payment and offer. A mismatch can delay bank payment.

## Nepal Document Preparation for Admission and Visa

Prepare clean scans of:

- Passport.
- Citizenship.
- SEE/SLC and NEB Class 11/12 documents.
- Bachelor's transcripts and degree documents for postgraduate.
- English test score.
- Marriage certificate if married.
- Relationship certificate for sponsors.
- Financial documents and source-of-income evidence.
- Work/training certificates if claiming experience or explaining a gap.

## Attestation and Translation

Many Australian providers accept clear English-issued documents directly, but some may ask for certified copies. If a document is in Nepali, translate it into English through a recognized translator/notary path. Keep originals available because providers, banks, NOC office, or visa processing may request verification.

## Bank Payment Flow

Common payment sequence from the local training material:

1. Receive offer letter.
2. Prepare NOC/NOL documents.
3. Receive payment approval from the family/sponsor and bank.
4. Pay tuition/deposit according to the provider's official invoice or offer acceptance instruction.
5. Keep Swift confirmation/payment receipt.
6. Provide payment proof, OSHC proof, and offer acceptance for CoE issuance.

Never send money to a personal account. Pay only through the provider's official payment method listed in the offer/invoice.

## Follow-Up Questions

- Have you received the offer letter?
- Does the offer show provider name, course, campus, intake, tuition, and CRICOS code?
- Is the payment being made from Nepal through a bank?
- Do your citizenship, passport, and transcript names match?
- Are you changing provider or course after NOC?
