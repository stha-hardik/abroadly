# Australia Financial Capacity, Sponsors, and Money Evidence

Financial evidence is one of the highest-risk parts of an Australia Student visa. Always distinguish between the minimum Home Affairs threshold and the student's real budget. As of 29 May 2026, Home Affairs says actual living costs may be much higher than the visa minimum and students should not rely on work to support themselves or family in Australia.

## Current Home Affairs Minimums

If Home Affairs requires financial capacity evidence, the student must be able to show at least:

- Travel costs: AUD 2,000 if applying from outside Australia outside Africa; AUD 1,000 if applying in Australia. Different Africa travel-cost guidance exists on the Home Affairs page.
- Course fees: first 12 months of fees, or pro-rata if the course is shorter than 12 months, minus amounts already paid with proof.
- Living costs for the student: AUD 29,710 for 12 months.
- Living costs for a partner coming with the student: AUD 10,394 for 12 months.
- Living costs for a child coming with the student: AUD 4,449 for 12 months.
- Schooling costs: at least AUD 13,502 per year for each school-age child, unless an official exemption applies.

These figures are current in the Home Affairs Student visa page text checked on 29 May 2026. Re-check before final visa submission.

## Annual Income Option

Home Affairs also lists an annual-income option. As of 29 May 2026:

- Parent or partner annual income: at least AUD 87,856 in the 12 months before applying.
- If bringing family members: at least AUD 102,500.

Home Affairs says evidence should be official government documents such as tax assessments less than 12 months old. It says bank statements or direct employer evidence are not accepted for this annual-income option.

## Acceptable Money Evidence Categories

Home Affairs examples include:

- Deposits of money from a financial institution.
- Government loans or loans from a financial institution.
- Scholarships or similar financial support.

The student must also show genuine access to the funds. If someone else provides money, provide relationship evidence, identity documents, and evidence of support.

## Nepal-Specific Financial Documents Often Used

From the local training checklist, Nepali students commonly prepare:

- Relationship certificate showing sponsor relationship.
- Sponsor citizenship/passport documents.
- Income documents with supporting proof.
- Salary certificate, payslips, and bank statements for employed sponsors.
- Business registration, PAN/VAT documents, tax/audit report, renewal, and business bank statements for business sponsors.
- House rent agreement and rent receipts/bank deposits for rental income.
- Land lease documents for lease income.
- Pension documents and pension bank statement for pension income.
- Vehicle rental agreement, blue book copy/translation, and income evidence for vehicle income.
- Bank balance certificate and transaction statement.
- Education loan sanction/disbursement documents if using a bank loan.

## Sponsor Logic

Strong sponsors are usually close family: parents, spouse, grandparents, siblings, and sometimes uncles/aunts/in-laws if relationship and genuine access are well documented. The farther the relationship, the more evidence is needed. Sudden unexplained deposits are weak. Home Affairs can ask how funds were accumulated.

## Warning Signs

- One-day bank balance certificate with no transaction history.
- Large unexplained deposits immediately before application.
- Sponsor relationship not proven.
- Business income claimed without registration/tax/bank support.
- Loan approved "in principle" but not actually available under the terms.
- Budget assumes part-time work will pay tuition.
