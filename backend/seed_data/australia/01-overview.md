# Studying in Australia — Overview for Nepali Students

Australia is a common destination for Nepali students because it combines English-medium study, CRICOS-regulated courses for international students, strong public universities, applied private higher education providers, TAFE and VET pathways, and temporary post-study work options for eligible graduates. Use this corpus for Australia questions from Nepali and South Asian students, especially after Class 12, bachelor-to-master progression, documents, finances, and Student visa subclass 500.

## How to Answer Australia Questions

Give a direct answer first, then ask for the missing profile details needed to make it specific: last qualification, percentage or GPA, English score, intended course, budget, preferred intake, preferred city, study gap, marital/dependent status, and whether the student has already received an offer or CoE.

When a rule changes often, say the date and point the student to the official source. As of 29 May 2026, the key official sources are the Department of Home Affairs for visas, Study Australia for official student guidance, CRICOS for course registration, TEQSA for provider registration, Fair Work for wages, and MoEST Nepal's NOC portal for No Objection Certificate/NOL.

Do not promise admission, scholarships, work, permanent residency, or visa approval. Do not say a consultancy is required. Students can apply directly to education providers and directly lodge visas through ImmiAccount.

## Why Australia Can Be a Good Fit

- Bachelor degrees are usually 3 years, or 4 years for honours, engineering, education, and some professional programs.
- Master's coursework programs are commonly 1.5 to 2 years. Research degrees have separate supervisor and proposal requirements.
- Most universities have February and July intakes; many private providers and pathway colleges also offer March, May, September, November, or trimester intakes.
- Student visa holders can generally work up to 48 hours per fortnight while the course is in session, with broader rights during scheduled course breaks.
- Study Australia provides official tools for course search, course fees, scholarships, location comparison, and cost-of-living planning.

## Trade-Offs

- Australia is expensive. For the Student visa, Home Affairs minimum financial capacity is only a visa threshold, not a real comfort budget. Sydney and Melbourne are typically higher cost than many regional cities.
- Work income should not be treated as the main funding source. Home Affairs explicitly expects students to have enough money for tuition, travel, and living costs before relying on work.
- Visa settings changed significantly in 2024-2026, including the Genuine Student requirement, English evidence, and Temporary Graduate subclass 485 streams. Older advice using "GTE" or older living-cost amounts should be treated as stale.
- Health, teaching, social work, nursing, psychology, and some education courses often require higher English scores and professional accreditation. A general IELTS 6.0 rule is not enough for these fields.

## Best-Fit Student Profiles

Australia is often suitable for:

- Class 12 graduates with a clear course match and enough family funding for at least the first year.
- Bachelor's graduates targeting master's in IT, data, business, professional accounting, engineering, public health, education, social work, counselling, or hospitality.
- Students who can explain why the chosen course is a logical progression from their academics, work, or career goal.
- Students who want a mix of study, part-time work exposure, and a regulated international student system.

Australia may be weaker for:

- Students whose only goal is permanent residency but who have no interest in the course.
- Students with unclear funds or sudden unexplained deposits.
- Students choosing a random course with no academic or career link.
- Students expecting part-time work to pay all tuition.
