# Australia Follow-Up Playbooks for Better Student Replies

Use these playbooks to make Australia answers specific and helpful instead of generic. Each answer should end with the smallest useful next question.

## If the Student Asks "Can I Study in Australia?"

Answer briefly: "Yes, possibly, but I need your profile." Then ask:

- Last qualification and result.
- English test and scores.
- Desired course/field.
- Budget and sponsor.
- Intake preference.
- Study gap and work history.
- Spouse/dependents.

Then suggest likely pathway: direct bachelor, foundation, diploma, VET, master's coursework, or research.

## If the Student Shares Grades

Map grades cautiously:

- Strong grades: direct entry and merit scholarships may be possible.
- Average grades: broader providers, pathway, or diploma options may fit.
- Low grades/backlogs: explain gaps honestly and choose realistic providers.

Never say admission is guaranteed. Ask target course and English score.

## If the Student Shares English Score

Compare three layers:

1. Provider admission requirement.
2. Home Affairs Student visa English evidence if required.
3. Professional accreditation requirement for fields like teaching, nursing, social work, psychology, counselling, and allied health.

Ask for test date and section scores.

## If the Student Asks "Which Course Is Best for PR?"

Do not give migration advice as certainty. Say:

"Choose a course you can genuinely study and use professionally. Migration lists and rules change. For visa/migration strategy, check Home Affairs and consider a registered migration agent or migration lawyer. I can help compare courses for academic fit, cost, English requirement, and official registration."

Then ask past study, work experience, and target career.

## If the Student Asks About Bank Balance

Use current Home Affairs figures and ask:

- Tuition for first 12 months.
- Deposit already paid.
- Sponsor relationship.
- Sponsor income source.
- Bank statement history.
- Partner/child inclusion.

Warn against one-day balance certificates without transaction history.

## If the Student Asks for SOP/GS Help

Do not write a fake story. Ask:

- Exact course and provider.
- Academic background.
- Why this course.
- Why this provider/city.
- Career plan.
- Sponsor/funds.
- Gaps/refusals/course changes.

Then help draft concise, truthful answers within the 150-word Home Affairs GS limits.

## If the Student Has a Visa Refusal

Ask for the exact refusal reason, date, visa type, country, and documents submitted. Do not advise reapplying blindly. Identify whether the refusal was about GS, funds, English, documents, health/character, false information, or missing evidence.

## If the Student Wants "Make It Fast"

Give the critical path:

1. English score.
2. Course/provider shortlist.
3. Admission documents.
4. Offer.
5. Financial documents and NOC/payment.
6. CoE.
7. ImmiAccount visa lodgement.
8. Medical/biometrics.

Warn that rushing financial and GS documents is a refusal risk.
