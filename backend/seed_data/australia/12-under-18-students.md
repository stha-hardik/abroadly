# Australia Students Under 18

Students under 18 have extra welfare and parental-consent requirements. Do not answer under-18 Australia questions with a normal adult checklist. Ask the student's date of birth, intended arrival date, course start date, accommodation plan, guardian plan, and provider welfare documents.

## Core Under-18 Requirement

Home Affairs says Student visa applicants under 18 must have adequate welfare arrangements in place while in Australia. If the student will be 18 when arriving, tell Home Affairs because some welfare evidence may not be needed.

## Welfare Evidence Options

Common welfare evidence pathways include:

- Confirmation of Appropriate Accommodation and Welfare, often called CAAW, arranged through the education provider.
- Acceptance Advice of Secondary Exchange Student form for secondary exchange students.
- Welfare approval from the relevant Minister for some government-sponsored students.
- Nominating a guardian where allowed, including Form 157N Student Guardianship Arrangements.

If a guardian is nominated, Home Affairs expects relationship proof, identity and residency evidence, police certificate where required, and a guardian who meets age and relationship/residency conditions.

## Parent/Guardian Consent

The visa application may need consent from parents or legal guardians for a child under 18 to travel and live in Australia. The local checklist mentioned birth certificate, parents' identity documents, signatures, and guardian documents. Form 1229 may be relevant for consent to grant an Australian visa to a child under 18, depending on the case and instructions.

## Practical Advice

- Do not book travel before welfare dates and OSHC dates are valid.
- The student must not arrive before approved accommodation/welfare begins.
- Accommodation should match the CAAW or guardian documents.
- Keep original birth certificate, parent/guardian citizenship/passport, and relationship documents ready.
- If one parent is unavailable, separated, deceased, or abroad, the student may need legal custody/consent evidence.

## Follow-Up Questions

- What is the student's date of birth and arrival date?
- Is the course school, foundation, diploma, VET, or university?
- Is the provider issuing CAAW?
- Will a parent/guardian travel with the student?
- Are both parents available to sign consent?
