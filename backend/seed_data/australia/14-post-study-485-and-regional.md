# Australia Temporary Graduate Visa 485 and Regional Study

The Temporary Graduate visa subclass 485 is for eligible international students who recently graduated from an Australian institution. Do not promise 485 eligibility before checking current Home Affairs rules, course registration, Australian study requirement, age, English, health insurance, AFP check, and visa timing.

## Current Post-Higher Education Work Stream Stay Periods

As of 29 May 2026, Home Affairs lists the Post-Higher Education Work stream stay periods as:

- Bachelor degree, including honours: 2 years.
- Master's coursework or extended: 2 years.
- Master's research: 3 years.
- Doctoral degree: 3 years.

Home Affairs says Hong Kong and British National Overseas passport holders may stay up to 5 years. Indian national stay periods differ under AI-ECTA; do not apply those Indian-national rules to Nepali students.

## Age Settings

Home Affairs says applicants generally must be 35 or under for Post-Higher Education Work stream. Exceptions can apply, including applicants using a master's research or doctoral degree, and Hong Kong/BNO passport holders, who may be under 50. Check the exact stream page before advising.

## Australian Study Requirement

For subclass 485 study requirement, Home Affairs says the applicant must have completed, in the 6 months immediately before applying, either a single eligible qualification requiring at least 2 academic years of study or multiple qualifications totalling at least 2 academic years resulting in an eligible qualification. The study must have been completed in Australia in no less than 16 calendar months, while holding a visa authorising study. Courses must be CRICOS-registered and taught in English.

Two academic years equals 92 weeks of registered study based on the CRICOS duration, not how long the student personally took. Failed subjects do not count.

## Regional and Second Stream

Some students may be eligible for a Second Post-Higher Education Work stream after studying and living in designated regional areas. Rules depend on where the first 485 was granted from, where the student studied/lived/worked, qualification, and current Home Affairs regional categories. Do not give a blanket "regional gives extra years" answer. Ask for campus location, residential address history, and current 485 stream.

## Common 485 Mistakes

- Course is not CRICOS-registered or not eligible.
- Course duration is less than 92 CRICOS weeks.
- Credits reduce actual Australian study below 16 calendar months.
- Applying more than 6 months after course completion.
- Missing AFP check or health insurance evidence at application.
- Assuming a short diploma automatically gives post-study work.
- Relying on old rules that gave longer master's coursework stays.
