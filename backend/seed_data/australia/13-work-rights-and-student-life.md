# Australia Work Rights, Wages, and Student Life

Student work rights are useful but should not be treated as the student's funding plan. Home Affairs says students should have enough money for their stay and should not rely on work to support themselves or family in Australia.

## Student Visa Work Rights

As of 29 May 2026, Home Affairs says:

- Student visa holders and their family members cannot work before the student's course starts.
- Student visa holders can work up to 48 hours per fortnight while the course is in session.
- Student visa holders can work unlimited hours when the course is not in session.
- If the student is studying a master's by research or doctoral degree, the student and family members can work more than 48 hours per fortnight after the course starts.
- If the student studies a master's by coursework or master's extended, family members can work more than 48 hours per fortnight, but the student remains capped at 48 hours per fortnight while the course is in session.

Always tell students to check their visa grant letter and VEVO because conditions can vary.

## Minimum Wage and Pay

Fair Work Ombudsman says that from 1 July 2025 the National Minimum Wage is AUD 24.95 per hour or AUD 948 per week before tax for employees not covered by an award or registered agreement. Many students work under awards in retail, hospitality, cleaning, aged care, childcare, or administration, and award rates can differ. Casual employees can have loading. Use the Fair Work Pay and Conditions Tool for exact pay.

## Common Student Jobs

Common entry jobs include hospitality, retail, supermarkets, cleaning, kitchen hand, barista, aged care assistant where qualified, disability support where qualified, tutoring, campus jobs, delivery under lawful employment classification, and admin. Warn students not to breach visa work limits or accept cash-in-hand underpayment.

## Cost-of-Living Guidance

Study Australia provides a cost-of-living calculator and says living costs vary by city, accommodation, transport, and lifestyle. Smaller cities can be cheaper, but job options and transport may be different. Budget before choosing campus.

## On Arrival Priorities

- Attend orientation and complete enrolment.
- Confirm OSHC card/access.
- Open a bank account.
- Apply for Tax File Number if working.
- Get a local SIM.
- Learn transport routes and student concession rules by state.
- Keep emergency contacts and provider support contacts.
- Know Fair Work rights and report unsafe or exploitative work.
