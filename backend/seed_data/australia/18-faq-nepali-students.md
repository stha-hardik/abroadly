# Australia Study FAQ for Nepali Students

Common questions Nepali students ask about Australia. Ask follow-up questions when the student's profile is missing.

## Eligibility and Admission

**Q: Can I study in Australia after Class 12?**
Yes. Common routes are direct bachelor, foundation, diploma-to-degree pathway, and VET/TAFE. The right route depends on Class 12 grades, English score, subject prerequisites, course, and budget.

**Q: I have 60 percent in +2. Can I get admission?**
Possibly, especially in many business, IT, hospitality, and pathway programs. Stronger universities and health/engineering/teaching courses may need higher marks and prerequisite subjects. Share your exact marks, subjects, English score, and target course.

**Q: I have a study gap. Is Australia possible?**
Yes, if the gap is explained and supported with work, training, family, health, or exam preparation evidence. A gap without documents weakens both admission and GS.

**Q: Can I change field from science to business or IT?**
Sometimes. You need a convincing academic/career reason. Random course changes can create GS risk.

## English

**Q: Is IELTS compulsory?**
Not always. Providers may accept IELTS, PTE Academic, TOEFL iBT, Cambridge, OET, or other approved tests. For visa English evidence, use Home Affairs-approved tests and score rules. Nepal does not get automatic passport-based English exemption.

**Q: PTE 47 is enough?**
For visa evidence, Home Affairs lists PTE Academic 47 as a minimum when evidence is required for tests taken on or after 7 August 2025. But provider admission may require higher, especially master's or professional fields.

## Finance and Visa

**Q: How much bank balance do I need for Australia?**
As of 29 May 2026, Home Affairs minimum financial capacity includes first 12 months tuition, travel, and living cost of AUD 29,710 for the student. Add AUD 10,394 for a partner, AUD 4,449 for a child, and schooling costs if school-age children apply. This is a minimum, not a comfortable real-life budget.

**Q: Can my parents sponsor me?**
Yes, if relationship, identity, income, savings/loan, and genuine access to funds are documented. For sponsors beyond parents/spouse, evidence must be stronger.

**Q: What is GS? Is it same as GTE?**
Use GS, Genuine Student. Old documents may say GTE, but current Home Affairs Student visa uses GS questions and supporting evidence.

**Q: Can anyone guarantee my Australia visa?**
No. Only Home Affairs decides. Anyone promising a guaranteed visa is misleading you.

## Work and Life

**Q: How many hours can I work?**
Generally 48 hours per fortnight while course is in session and unlimited hours in scheduled breaks. Research master's and doctoral students have different work-limit settings. Check the grant letter and VEVO.

**Q: Can part-time work pay my tuition?**
Do not plan that way. Part-time work can help living expenses, but Home Affairs expects funds before visa grant and says students should not rely on work to support themselves or family.

## Post-Study

**Q: How many years post-study work after bachelor or master?**
As of 29 May 2026, the 485 Post-Higher Education Work stream lists bachelor 2 years, master's coursework/extended 2 years, master's research 3 years, and doctoral 3 years. Eligibility still depends on current rules.

**Q: Does regional study guarantee PR?**
No. Regional study may affect some post-study or migration settings, but it does not guarantee permanent residency.

## Documents

**Q: What documents do I need for Australia admission?**
Passport, academics, English test, SOP/GS questionnaire if required, CV, gap/work evidence, LOR for many master's, marriage certificate if relevant, and application forms/portfolio/research proposal when required.

**Q: What is NOC/NOL?**
It is Nepal's No Objection Certificate/Letter from MoEST, usually needed to send tuition fees abroad through a Nepali bank. Official portal: https://noc.moest.gov.np/.
