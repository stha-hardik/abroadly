# Australia Field-Specific Guidance

Use field-specific caution when students ask "which course is best?" The correct answer depends on academic background, English, budget, career goal, and accreditation. Do not recommend a course only because admission is easy.

## IT, Cyber Security, Data Science

Good for students with math, computer, science, engineering, business analytics, or strong self-study projects. Ask about programming, math, statistics, and whether the student wants software, cyber, data, cloud, AI, or business analytics. Check if the course accepts non-IT graduates.

## Accounting, Business, Project Management

Good for commerce, management, accounting, finance, and general business profiles. For professional accounting, check CPA Australia, CA ANZ, and IPA accreditation if the student's goal depends on professional recognition. Project management should be linked to prior business, engineering, IT, construction, or operations experience.

## Engineering

Requires careful prerequisite and accreditation checks. Ask whether the course is Engineering Honours, Engineering Science, Engineering Technology, or a master's conversion/specialization. Check Engineers Australia alignment where relevant.

## Nursing, Allied Health, Medical Laboratory, Public Health

High English and placement requirements are common. Nursing and allied health can be competitive and expensive. Ask about biology/science background, English bands, clinical placement readiness, immunisation, police checks, and professional registration.

## Social Work, Counselling, Psychology, Community Services

Social work and counselling can require high English and field placements. Psychology pathways can be long and require accredited sequences for registration. Community services VET/higher education should match the student's real work goals.

## Teaching and Early Childhood

Teaching and early childhood often require strong English, professional accreditation, child-safety checks, and placements. Ask whether the course leads to teacher registration or only education studies.

## Hospitality, Tourism, Cookery

Suitable for practical-service careers when the student understands the workload, placements, and realistic outcomes. Avoid presenting hospitality as a shortcut to migration. Check CRICOS, provider quality, fee, campus, and progression options.

## Creative Arts, Design, Film, Audio, Games

Ask for portfolio, software skills, work samples, and career goal. Creative courses can be excellent when the student has genuine interest and evidence. They are weak choices when selected only because entry seems flexible.

## Research Degrees

For master's research or PhD, ask for research area, supervisor match, proposal, publications, academic grades, and funding. Research applications take longer and are not the same as coursework applications.
