# Australia Scholarships and Budgeting

Australia scholarships exist, but most Nepali students should plan as if scholarships reduce tuition rather than fully fund the whole plan. Full government scholarships are competitive and often have leadership, development, academic, and return-home expectations.

## Scholarship Types

- Australia Awards: prestigious Australian Government scholarships for selected developing countries and priority profiles. Eligibility, participating countries, fields, and return obligations must be checked on the official Australia Awards site and Study Australia.
- Research Training Program: supports research master's and doctoral study through participating universities. Applications are normally through universities.
- Education provider merit scholarships: common partial tuition reductions such as 10-30 percent in many provider examples, with some higher awards. These are usually based on academic merit and may require maintaining grades.
- Regional, state, or city scholarships: some smaller cities, state governments, and providers offer regional bursaries or incentives.
- Course-specific scholarships: health, STEM, business, women in STEM, leadership, and international merit awards.

## Budget Components

A realistic Australia budget includes:

- Application fees if charged by the provider.
- English test and document costs.
- First tuition deposit or first semester fee.
- OSHC for the visa period.
- Student visa application charge and additional applicant charges if family applies.
- Medicals and biometrics if required.
- NOC/NOL and bank transfer costs in Nepal.
- Airfare.
- Initial accommodation bond, first rent, basic setup, transport, SIM, food, and emergency buffer.
- Remaining tuition instalments.

## Use Current Visa Financial Minimums

As of 29 May 2026, Home Affairs minimum living-cost threshold is AUD 29,710 for the primary student, plus tuition, travel, and family costs if applicable. This is not a real comfort budget. Sydney and Melbourne can exceed the minimum quickly depending on rent.

## Scholarship Answer Pattern

When asked "Can I get scholarship in Australia?", answer:

1. Yes, but it depends on grades, course, provider, intake, English, and scholarship deadline.
2. Ask for GPA/percentage, level, course, and budget.
3. Suggest searching Study Australia Course Search and provider scholarship pages.
4. Warn that scholarships rarely replace the need for strong financial evidence.

## Budget Red Flags

- Student plans to borrow only after visa.
- Family can pay deposit but not later tuition.
- Student expects part-time work to cover tuition.
- Scholarship is assumed but not awarded in writing.
- Living budget ignores rent bond, OSHC, airfare, and first-month setup.
