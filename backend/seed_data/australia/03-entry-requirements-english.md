# Australia Academic and English Entry Requirements

Australia has two separate English checks: education-provider admission English and, where required, Student visa English evidence. A student can satisfy a university's admission rule but still need to meet Home Affairs evidence rules, or vice versa. Always separate "provider admission" from "visa evidence."

## Provider Admission English Patterns

The training spreadsheets show common provider patterns:

- Many undergraduate courses: IELTS 6.0 overall with no band below 5.5 or 6.0, or PTE around 50-52 with section minimums.
- Many master's coursework courses: IELTS 6.5 overall with no band below 6.0, or PTE around 58 with section minimums around 50.
- Teaching, social work, nursing, counselling, psychology, speech pathology, occupational therapy, and some health courses: often IELTS 7.0 with strict bands or equivalent. Do not generalize these as IELTS 6.5 courses.
- Some providers accept IELTS, PTE Academic, TOEFL iBT, Cambridge C1 Advanced, OET for health, or approved alternatives, but each course page decides.

If a student says "I have PTE 50," answer by course type:

- Business/IT bachelor might be possible at some providers.
- Master's generally may need higher PTE.
- Nursing/social work/teaching likely needs substantially higher English.

## Student Visa English Evidence

As of 29 May 2026, Home Affairs lists different accepted score tables depending on when the test was taken.

For tests taken on or after 7 August 2025, Home Affairs lists these minimum Student visa evidence scores where evidence is required:

- IELTS Academic or General Training: 6.0 minimum; 5.5 with at least 10 weeks ELICOS/foundation/eligible pathway; 5.0 with at least 20 weeks ELICOS.
- PTE Academic: 47 minimum; 39 with at least 10 weeks ELICOS/foundation/eligible pathway; 31 with at least 20 weeks ELICOS.
- TOEFL iBT: 67 minimum; 51 with at least 10 weeks ELICOS/foundation/eligible pathway; 37 with at least 20 weeks ELICOS.
- C1 Advanced: 161 minimum; Home Affairs excludes lower pathway scores for this test.
- CELPIP General, LANGUAGECERT Academic, MET, and OET also appear in the Home Affairs table.

For TOEFL iBT from 21 January 2026, Home Affairs says applicants must select "Taking TOEFL for Australia" during registration for the test to be valid for Australian visa purposes. Home Affairs does not accept fully online or at-home tests for visa English evidence.

## Test Validity and Exemptions

Home Affairs generally requires approved test results within the relevant 2-year window. Some students may be exempt from giving visa English evidence depending on passport, course type, government sponsorship, postgraduate research, school course, ELICOS-only, or specified prior study in English-speaking countries. Nepal is not a majority-English passport country for automatic exemption.

## Academic Entry Patterns from Local Course Data

The course workbook examples commonly used:

- Undergraduate: around 2.65-3.0 GPA, 60-70 percent, or "best four subjects" calculations for some institutions.
- Postgraduate: completed bachelor degree, often around 55-60 percent or 2.4-2.8 GPA depending on provider.
- Research degrees: supervisor approval, research proposal, and stronger academic/research fit.
- IT/engineering: mathematics or relevant prior study may matter.
- Health/education/social work: extra English and placement requirements can dominate the admission decision.

Use these as practical patterns, not official cutoffs. Always check the target provider's Nepal-specific entry page or course page.

## Follow-Up Questions to Ask

- Is your English score IELTS, PTE, TOEFL, OET, Cambridge, or another test?
- What is the test date?
- What is the overall score and each band/section score?
- Is your target course undergraduate, master's, ELICOS, foundation, pathway, or research?
- Is the course in teaching, nursing, social work, psychology, or health?
