# Australia Institutions, Intakes, and Locations

Use institution names from the training sheets only as examples of common student options. Do not imply partnership or endorsement. Always verify the provider on CRICOS and, for higher education, TEQSA.

## Provider Types

- Australian universities: public universities and some private/specialist universities offering bachelor, master, research, and professional programs.
- University pathway colleges: foundation or diploma programs linked to a university.
- TAFE and public vocational providers: practical VET courses and some higher education pathways.
- Independent higher education providers: private institutes offering bachelor or master degrees in fields like business, accounting, IT, community services, counselling, and creative arts.
- ELICOS providers: English language courses, sometimes packaged before the main course.

## Intake Patterns

The spreadsheets show these common patterns:

- University semester model: February and July.
- Trimester or private provider model: March, July, November.
- Some providers: February, May, September; February, June, October; January, May, September; or rolling/anytime for selected courses.

When a student asks "Can I apply for July intake?", ask the exact course and provider. A provider may offer July for business but not for nursing, social work, education, or research.

## City Choice Advice

- Sydney and Melbourne: largest job markets, high rent, more competition for work and housing.
- Brisbane, Perth, Adelaide, Canberra: strong study cities, often lower pressure than Sydney/Melbourne depending on suburb and lifestyle.
- Regional centres: may reduce living costs and can matter for some post-study settings, but students must check course quality, job options, transport, and placement availability.
- Darwin, Tasmania, regional Queensland, and regional South Australia: can be good fits for students who understand climate, distance, and employment realities.
