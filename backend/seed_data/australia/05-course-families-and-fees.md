# Australia Course Families, Fees, and Practical Course Advice

The local training workbook contained 2,563 course rows across bachelor and master's options. The rows were strongest for practical course families: business/accounting, IT/cyber/data, engineering, health/social work/education, creative industries, hospitality/tourism, and sciences. Use this file to answer broad course-selection questions, not to quote a fixed fee without checking the current provider page.

## Fee Patterns from the Training Workbook

The workbook's numeric fee examples ranged roughly from AUD 10,000 to AUD 79,300, with a median around AUD 35,826 among extracted numeric values. Many private provider and pathway examples sat around AUD 13,500-AUD 30,800 per year. Many university examples sat higher, commonly in the AUD 31,000-AUD 45,000+ range. Medicine, some health, engineering, and elite university courses can be much higher.

Do not tell a student "Australia costs exactly X." Say: tuition depends on provider, course, campus, scholarship, and annual fee increases. For budgeting, combine first-year tuition, Home Affairs living-cost threshold, travel, OSHC, visa costs, medical/biometrics, and emergency buffer.

## Business, Accounting, Management, Project Management

Common options: Bachelor of Business, Bachelor of Commerce, Bachelor of Accounting, Master of Business Administration, Master of Professional Accounting, Master of Project Management, Master of Business Analytics.

Good fit: students with commerce, management, accounting, finance, economics, business, or broad career goals. Ask whether the student needs professional accounting accreditation and whether the course includes CPA/CA/IPA alignment.

## IT, Cyber Security, Data, Software

Common options: Bachelor of Information Technology, Bachelor of Computer Science, Bachelor of Software Engineering, Master of Information Technology, Master of Cyber Security, Master of Data Science, Master of Business Analytics.

Good fit: students with math, computer, science, engineering, or quantitative background. For students without IT background, ask whether the master's accepts non-IT graduates and whether it includes foundation units.

## Engineering and Technology

Common options: engineering honours, engineering science, engineering technology, renewable energy, manufacturing, civil, electrical, mechanical, computer systems, chemical, petroleum.

Important checks: math/science prerequisites, Engineers Australia accreditation, work-integrated learning, lab/campus location, and whether the course is bachelor/honours or technology/associate level.

## Health, Nursing, Social Work, Education, Psychology

Common options: nursing practice, public health, occupational therapy, medical laboratory science, social work, early childhood education, primary/secondary teaching, counselling, psychology.

These courses are sensitive. They often have higher English requirements, placements, immunisation/police checks, professional accreditation, and limited intakes. Teaching and social work frequently require IELTS around 7.0 with strict bands or equivalent; do not assume normal business/IT English thresholds apply.

## Creative Industries, Design, Media, Film, Audio, Games

Common options: animation, music, audio, film, games, creative arts, design, architecture, media and communication.

Ask for portfolio requirements, equipment/software costs, campus facilities, and graduate outcomes. Creative courses should be selected because the student has work samples or a serious creative goal, not only because admission is easier.

## Hospitality, Tourism, Community Services, Early Childhood

These appear often in private/VET and some university pathways. Ask whether the course is VET, higher education, or a bachelor. Also ask whether placements are required and whether the qualification aligns with the student's real career plan.
