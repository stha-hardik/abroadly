# Australia Student Visa Subclass 500

The Student visa subclass 500 is the usual visa for international students studying a full-time CRICOS-registered course in Australia. As of 29 May 2026, Home Affairs says students can apply online in or outside Australia, must hold a valid Confirmation of Enrolment when the application is decided, must hold OSHC unless exempt, must be at least 6 years old, and must have welfare arrangements if under 18.

## Core Requirements

- Valid passport.
- Confirmation of Enrolment for every intended course.
- Full-time enrolment in a CRICOS-registered course.
- Genuine Student responses and supporting documents.
- English evidence if required by the Document Checklist tool or requested by Home Affairs.
- Financial capacity evidence if required or requested.
- OSHC for the proposed stay.
- Health and character requirements.
- Biometrics and medical examinations if requested.
- Family documents if partner or children are included or declared.

## CoE and Packaged Courses

The CoE is critical. Home Affairs says a Student visa application can be invalid if the required CoE is not provided at lodgement. If applying for a package of courses, include all CoE codes. Course gaps must generally be less than two calendar months unless the first course finishes at the end of the standard academic year and the next begins at the start of the standard academic year.

The last course in the package is the main course of study. Home Affairs uses the main course to work out some English and financial evidence settings.

## Visa Application Charge

Do not guess the exact visa fee in student answers. Home Affairs displays visa pricing dynamically and tells applicants to use the Visa Pricing Estimator. Say: "Check the Visa Pricing Estimator on the day you apply; the charge can change and family members may add charges." Also mention separate costs: health checks, biometrics, police certificates if needed, OSHC, translations, bank charges, and travel.

## Current Work Rights on Student Visa

Home Affairs says Student visa holders can work up to 48 hours per fortnight while the course is in session. They can work unlimited hours during scheduled course breaks. Students studying a master's by research or doctoral degree, and their families, have no work-hour limit after the course starts. Family-work rights vary by the student's course level; check the visa grant letter and VEVO.

## Applying in Australia

Students can apply in or outside Australia, but not every visa allows an onshore Student visa application. Home Affairs lists excluded visas, including Visitor subclass 600, ETA subclass 601, eVisitor subclass 651, Transit subclass 771, Temporary Graduate subclass 485, and others. If the student is already in Australia, ask what visa they hold before advising.

## Refusal Risk Areas

- Missing or invalid CoE.
- Weak Genuine Student explanation or course mismatch.
- Insufficient or unexplained funds.
- No OSHC details.
- English evidence missing when required.
- False or inconsistent information.
- Name/date mismatch across documents.
- Applying with an ineligible onshore visa.
