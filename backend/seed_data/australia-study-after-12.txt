# Studying in Australia after Class 12 in Nepal

## Overview
Nepali students can pursue a Bachelor's degree in Australia directly after completing Class 12 (NEB, A-Level, or IB). Australia is the third most popular destination for international students globally. Australian universities recognise Nepali Class 12 (NEB) as broadly equivalent to Year 12 in Australia for most undergraduate programs.

## Entry Pathways

There are three main routes from Nepali Class 12 to an Australian bachelor's degree.

1. Direct Entry to Bachelor's Degree (3-4 years). Students with strong Class 12 results, typically 60% or higher in NEB or equivalent, can apply directly. Bachelor's degrees take 3 years for most fields, 4 years for engineering, law, or honours programs, and 5-6 years for medicine and dentistry.

2. Foundation Year plus Bachelor's (1 year plus 3 years). Universities including Monash, UNSW, Sydney, and Western Australia offer foundation programs for students whose Class 12 results don't meet direct entry requirements. After successful completion, students progress to the first year of bachelor's.

3. Diploma plus Bachelor's (1 year plus 2 years). Diplomas at affiliated colleges like Monash College and UTS College lead directly into the second year of a bachelor's program at the partner university.

## Academic Requirements

- Minimum 60% in NEB Class 12 for most Australian universities
- 70% or higher typically required for Group of Eight universities (Melbourne, Sydney, ANU, UNSW, Queensland, Monash, Adelaide, Western Australia)
- Subject prerequisites apply for science, engineering, and health programs - typically Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, or Biology in Class 12
- Medicine, dentistry, and veterinary medicine require UCAT or ISAT tests and interviews

## English Language Requirements

- IELTS Academic: minimum overall band 6.0 to 6.5, with no individual band below 6.0 (varies by university and course)
- Alternatives accepted: TOEFL iBT 70-90, PTE Academic 50-65, Cambridge English C1 Advanced
- Some universities offer English Language Bridging or ELICOS programs for students whose scores fall slightly short

## Costs (annual estimates in Australian Dollars)

- Tuition for undergraduate programs: AUD 25,000 to 45,000 per year
- Medicine and dentistry: can exceed AUD 70,000 per year
- Living expenses: AUD 24,505 per year (Department of Home Affairs minimum for student visa as of 2024)
- Major cities (Sydney, Melbourne) are more expensive than Adelaide, Perth, or Brisbane
- Total estimated cost: AUD 50,000 to 70,000 per year all-in

## Student Visa (Subclass 500)

Required documents for the Australian Student visa:
- Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) issued by the university
- Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE) statement explaining study intent and ties to Nepal
- Financial proof showing capacity to cover tuition, AUD 24,505 living expenses for 12 months, and AUD 2,500 travel allowance
- Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the full visa duration
- IELTS or other English language test results
- Academic transcripts and certificates attested by the Ministry of Education, Government of Nepal
- No-objection certificate from the Ministry of Education
- Statement of Purpose

Visa application fee: approximately AUD 710 as of 2024 (subject to change).

## Application Timeline

- Apply 6 to 12 months before the intended intake
- Two main intakes: February (Semester 1) and July (Semester 2)
- Some courses also offer a November intake
- All Nepali academic documents must be attested by the Ministry of Education and notarised
- IELTS test should be taken at least 3-4 months before application

## Working Rights During Study

- 48 hours per fortnight during academic terms
- Unlimited hours during scheduled breaks and holidays
- Minimum wage in Australia is AUD 24.10 per hour (2024)
- Many students work part-time in hospitality, retail, or campus jobs

## Post-Study Work Rights

After graduation, eligible students receive a Temporary Graduate visa (Subclass 485):
- Bachelor's degree: 2 years of post-study work rights, extended by 1-2 years for regional study
- Master's by coursework: 3 years (5 years if studied in regional areas)
- PhD: 4 years (6 years if studied in regional areas)

## Scholarships

- Australia Awards Scholarships - fully funded, very competitive, primarily for postgraduate
- Destination Australia Scholarship - for studying in regional Australia, AUD 15,000 per year
- University-specific merit scholarships - typically 10-50% tuition reduction
- Vice-Chancellor's International Scholarship at various universities
- Many private and university awards specifically for South Asian students

## Important Tips for Nepali Students

- Start IELTS preparation early; many students need 2-3 attempts to reach 6.5
- Document attestation from MoE Nepal can take 2-4 weeks - plan ahead
- The GTE statement is critical - explain genuine study intent, family ties, and post-study plans
- Many Nepali students find it helpful to apply through registered education agents in Kathmandu
- Australian Embassy contact in Nepal: services are handled through the Australian High Commission in New Delhi for visa processing
