# Studying in the UK after Class 12 in Nepal

## Overview
The United Kingdom is one of the top destinations for Nepali students. UK universities including Oxford, Cambridge, Imperial College London, UCL, and Edinburgh consistently rank among the world's best. Nepali students can pursue undergraduate study in the UK after Class 12 (NEB, A-Level, or IB) through several entry pathways.

## Entry Pathways

1. Foundation Year plus Bachelor's (1 year plus 3 years). The most common route for Nepali students. Foundation programs prepare students academically, linguistically, and culturally for UK undergraduate study. Available at most universities directly and at international colleges like Kaplan, INTO, Study Group, and Navitas.

2. International Year One (1 year, then enter Year 2 of bachelor's). A faster alternative to foundation. After successful completion, students enter the second year of a bachelor's degree. Available at select universities including Coventry, Hertfordshire, and Brunel.

3. Direct Entry to Bachelor's Degree (3 years; 4 years in Scotland or for sandwich courses with industry placement). Available at universities that accept Nepali Class 12 directly, usually requiring 70% or higher in NEB. More common at post-1992 universities than Russell Group institutions.

## Academic Requirements

- Minimum 60-70% in NEB Class 12 for most UK universities
- Russell Group universities (the top 24 research-intensive universities including Oxford, Cambridge, Imperial, UCL, Edinburgh, Manchester) typically expect 80% or higher in NEB, or strong A-Level / IB scores
- Subject prerequisites apply for STEM, medicine, dentistry, and law
- Medicine, dentistry, and veterinary medicine usually require UCAT or BMAT tests and panel interviews

## English Language Requirements

- IELTS Academic: minimum overall 6.0 to 6.5 (some programs require 7.0 or higher), with no individual band below 5.5 to 6.0 - exact requirements vary by university and course
- Alternatives accepted: TOEFL iBT 80-100, PTE Academic 56-70, Cambridge English C1 Advanced
- IELTS must be UKVI-approved (taken at an approved test centre) for visa purposes
- Universities may offer pre-sessional English courses (4-12 weeks) for students just below the requirement

## Application Process

- Most UK universities require applications through UCAS (Universities and Colleges Admissions Service)
- One UCAS application allows up to 5 university choices
- Application deadlines: 31 January for most courses; 15 October for Oxford, Cambridge, medicine, dentistry, and veterinary medicine
- UCAS application fee: £28.50 for multiple choices as of 2024
- Personal statement required: 4,000 character limit (about 47 lines), explaining motivation and suitability
- Academic reference letter from school principal or teacher
- Some universities accept direct applications (especially for postgraduate)

## Costs (annual estimates in British Pounds)

- Tuition for undergraduate programs: £15,000 to £30,000 per year
- Medicine, dentistry: can exceed £40,000 per year
- Living expenses required by UK Home Office: £12,006 per year outside London, £15,609 per year in London (2024)
- Total estimated cost: £25,000 to £45,000 per year all-in

## Student Visa

Required documents for the UK Student visa:
- Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) issued by the university
- Financial proof: tuition for first year plus living costs for 9 months (£9,207 outside London, £12,006 in London) - funds must be held in the bank account for 28 consecutive days
- UKVI-approved English language test
- Academic transcripts and certificates attested by the Ministry of Education, Government of Nepal
- Tuberculosis (TB) test certificate from an IOM-approved clinic in Nepal
- Passport valid for the full duration of stay
- ATAS clearance for certain science and engineering programs
- Photograph meeting UK visa specifications

Visa application fee: £490 as of 2024, plus Immigration Health Surcharge of £776 per year of stay (the IHS gives access to NHS healthcare).

## Working Rights During Study

- 20 hours per week during term-time
- Full-time work allowed during official vacation periods
- Many degree programs include internships or industry placements
- Students must not be self-employed or work as professional sportspeople / entertainers

## Post-Study Work (Graduate Route)

After graduating from a UK university, international students can stay in the UK for 2 years (3 years for PhD graduates) on the Graduate Route visa without needing a job offer first. This visa allows working at any skill level for any employer.

## Scholarships

- Chevening Scholarships - fully funded, but for Master's only (not undergraduate)
- Commonwealth Scholarships - varies by program
- GREAT Scholarships for India and South Asia
- University-specific merit scholarships ranging from 10% to 50% tuition reduction
- Many universities offer South Asia or Nepal-specific scholarships
- Charity-funded scholarships through trusts like Inlaks Shivdasani Foundation (for postgraduate, but worth knowing)

## Important Tips for Nepali Students

- All Nepali academic documents must be attested by the Ministry of Education, Government of Nepal before submission to the UK visa office
- Bank statements for financial proof should be from a recognised bank with funds held for at least 28 consecutive days
- The British Embassy in Kathmandu handles UK visa applications (VFS Global is the visa application centre)
- Pre-departure briefings are offered by the British Council Nepal
- Start the UCAS application 8-10 months before the intake (September for autumn intake; January is typical deadline)
- Foundation year tuition is sometimes lower than direct bachelor entry - factor this into cost comparisons
- After Brexit, EU/EEA students are now also considered international, so competition for places has not eased significantly
