# UK Study — Frequently Asked Questions from Nepali Students

Common questions Nepali students ask, with grounded answers. Where Hinglish/Nepali-romanized phrasing is common, both phrasings are included so the retriever can match either.

## Eligibility and academics

**Q: Can I apply to UK universities with backlogs in my +2 / bachelor's?**
Most UK universities accept students with backlogs at the bachelor's level if you've passed all subjects by the time you graduate. For postgraduate, **0–2 backlogs at undergraduate is generally fine** for most universities; **3–5 is borderline** and depends on the university; **6+ is hard**. Russell Group universities are stricter — check the specific course page. Source: each university's "International qualifications" page (e.g., https://www.manchester.ac.uk/study/international/admissions/).

**Q: I have a study gap of 2-3 years. Can I still apply?**
Yes. UK universities are generally open to study gaps if you can show what you did during the gap (work, family responsibility, additional certification). Address it directly in your personal statement / SOP. Visa officers ask about gaps in credibility interviews — be ready with a clear answer.

**Q: My +2 percentage is 65%. Which universities can I get into?**
Below Russell Group, comfortably yes — Tier 3 universities (Coventry, Hertfordshire, Northumbria, Greenwich, Westminster, De Montfort, etc.) regularly admit students at 65%+. Some Russell Group universities require 70%+ for direct entry; foundation year is an alternative. Check the destination university's Nepal-specific page.

**Q: Ma backlog 4 wata cha. UK janu sakcha ki nai?** *(I have 4 backlogs — can I go to UK?)*
You can, especially for postgraduate. Apply to mid-tier universities (Tier 3 + some Tier 2) — they're far more flexible than the Russell Group. Be honest about it in your SOP.

## English language

**Q: Is IELTS the only test accepted?**
No. UK universities accept IELTS, TOEFL iBT, PTE Academic, and Cambridge English (CAE/CPE). But for the **visa**, you must take a SELT-approved test — usually IELTS for UKVI or PTE Academic UKVI. Confirm at https://www.gov.uk/guidance/prove-your-english-language-abilities-with-a-secure-english-language-test-selt.

**Q: Can I get an IELTS waiver if my medium of instruction was English?**
Usually no for Nepal. UKVI lists specific majority-English-speaking countries whose qualifications waive the SELT requirement — Nepal is not on that list [Source: GOV.UK SELT exemptions — https://www.gov.uk/guidance/prove-your-english-language-abilities-with-a-secure-english-language-test-selt]. Some universities accept "Medium of Instruction" certificates for **admission** but the **visa** still requires IELTS for UKVI.

**Q: IELTS 5.5 le UK ma chai kun university paunchu?** *(With IELTS 5.5, which UK university can I get?)*
With 5.5 you generally need a **pre-sessional English course** (a 6–24 week intensive English course before the main degree). Most UK universities offer this. After pre-sessional, you progress to the main degree without retaking IELTS. Check at the university's "Pre-sessional English" page.

## Finances

**Q: How much money do I need to show for a UK student visa?**
- **First year tuition** (as on your CAS, minus any deposit paid)
- **PLUS 9 months of living costs at the UKVI rate:**
  - **London:** £1,529 × 9 = £13,761
  - **Outside London:** £1,171 × 9 = £10,539
[Source: GOV.UK Student visa: money — https://www.gov.uk/student-visa/money]

For most students this is **£25,000–£40,000 total** depending on course + location.

**Q: Can my parents' bank account be used?**
Yes — your parent's account is allowed, with a **parental consent letter** and your **birth certificate** showing the relationship [Source: GOV.UK Student visa: money — https://www.gov.uk/student-visa/money]. Friends, uncles, aunts, or other relatives are **not** acceptable as fund holders.

**Q: Can I take an education loan from a Nepali bank?**
Yes. NIC Asia, Nabil, Sanima, Standard Chartered, and most commercial banks offer education loans for UK study. The loan letter must be on bank letterhead, signed and stamped, and state the loan is approved and available for study in the UK. Check individual bank loan pages (file 06 has links). For visa, the loan must be in your name (not "approved in principle").

**Q: Can my friend send money for funds?**
**No.** Only your own account or a parent's account is accepted. A friend's bank statement will get your visa refused.

**Q: How much real money do I need per month in the UK?**
Realistic monthly spending: **£1,800–£2,500 in London**, **£1,200–£1,800 outside London**. The UKVI maintenance figure is the minimum proof, not the actual lived cost.

## Visa

**Q: What is the UK Student visa fee?**
**£558** to apply from outside the UK [Source: GOV.UK Student visa — https://www.gov.uk/student-visa]. **Plus Immigration Health Surcharge (~£776 per year)**.

**Q: How long does the UK student visa take?**
Usually **within 3 weeks** of biometrics from outside the UK [Source: GOV.UK Student visa — https://www.gov.uk/student-visa]. Peak (June–September) often runs 4–6 weeks. Priority and Super-Priority services available for additional fee.

**Q: Will I have a visa interview?**
There is no compulsory consular interview. UKVI may call you for a **credibility interview** (phone or video) on a sample basis — be ready. See file 05 for what they ask.

**Q: My visa was refused. Can I reapply?**
Yes — there's no automatic ban. Fix the issue cited in the refusal letter (often funds or credibility), then submit a fresh application with the £558 fee. You can also request an **Administrative Review** within 28 days if you believe the refusal was a factual error (£80).

**Q: How early can I apply for my visa?**
**6 months before** your course start date is the earliest [Source: GOV.UK Student visa — https://www.gov.uk/student-visa].

**Q: Visa kati din ma aaucha?** *(How many days for the visa to arrive?)*
Usually within 21 days after biometrics; peak season 28–42 days.

## Family / dependents

**Q: Can I bring my spouse and children?**
Since January 2024, **dependants are restricted**. Only students on **postgraduate research courses (PhD)** or **government-sponsored scholarships of 6+ months** can bring dependants on a Student visa [Source: GOV.UK Student visa your partner and children — https://www.gov.uk/student-visa/your-partner-and-children]. A regular bachelor's or master's student **cannot bring dependants**.

**Q: Can my parents visit me during my studies?**
Yes — they apply for a **Standard Visitor visa** (£127, valid up to 6 months) [Source: GOV.UK Standard Visitor visa — https://www.gov.uk/standard-visitor]. They'll need to show ties to Nepal and funds for the visit.

## Working

**Q: How many hours can I work in the UK?**
**20 hours per week during term**, full-time during vacations, if your course is at degree level [Source: GOV.UK Student visa work — https://www.gov.uk/student-visa/work]. See file 08 for details.

**Q: Can I do Uber or Deliveroo?**
Most gig platforms classify workers as self-employed. **Self-employment is generally not allowed on a Student visa.** Verify your specific employment classification before signing up. PAYE part-time work (Tesco, Costa, etc.) is fine within the hour limit.

**Q: Can I find a job and stay after my studies?**
Yes — the **Graduate Route visa** gives you **2 years of unrestricted work** after a bachelor's/master's (3 years for PhD) if you apply on or before 31 December 2026. From 1 January 2027, the bachelor's/master's Graduate Route reduces to **18 months**. See file 09 for the full post-study story. [Source: GOV.UK Graduate visa — https://www.gov.uk/graduate-visa]

## Practical living

**Q: When should I book accommodation?**
For September entry, accommodation applications usually open **March–April**. Apply by **late June** to guarantee university halls. Private rental can be found later but is more expensive in London.

**Q: Will I get NHS coverage?**
Yes — once you've paid the **Immigration Health Surcharge** (about £776 per year for students), you get the same NHS coverage as UK residents [Source: GOV.UK IHS — https://www.gov.uk/healthcare-immigration-application].

**Q: Can I open a UK bank account before arriving?**
Some banks (HSBC, Lloyds) offer pre-arrival international student accounts. Most students open accounts after arriving with their BRP/eVisa and university enrolment letter (1-2 weeks).

**Q: How much does a winter jacket cost in the UK?**
£40–£150 in Primark, M&S, Sports Direct. Buy on arrival — Nepal jackets are usually not warm enough for UK winters (October–March).

## Returning to Nepal

**Q: Do I have to come back to Nepal after my studies?**
No, the UK does not require it. Returning is your choice. Coming back home after graduating is fully legitimate — many students take experience back to Nepali jobs in development, government, finance, healthcare, or family business.

**Q: If I take Chevening, do I have to return to Nepal?**
Yes — Chevening scholars commit to **returning to Nepal for a minimum of 2 years** after the scholarship ends [Source: Chevening — https://www.chevening.org/scholarship/eligibility/]. Other scholarships have varying return obligations — check yours.

## Anti-scam

**Q: A consultancy says they can guarantee my visa. Should I pay them?**
**No.** No agent or consultancy can guarantee a visa — the decision is solely with UKVI. Anyone promising "guaranteed visa" or charging large fees for "visa processing" is misleading you. You can submit the visa application yourself at https://www.gov.uk/student-visa/apply. The university issues the CAS for free.

**Q: A consultancy is asking for NPR 2 lakh for "complete UK package." Is this normal?**
Consultancies sometimes charge for help, but **all the work they do can be done by you**: shortlist universities → apply directly → upload documents → take IELTS → apply for visa. Fees that high typically don't include any service the official sources don't already provide for free. Compare what they offer against the free GOV.UK + university websites before paying.
