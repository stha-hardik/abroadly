# UK Application Timeline for Nepali Students

UK academic year has two main intakes (September the main one, January smaller). This timeline assumes you're targeting **September** entry. Counting backwards from "I want to start in September of year Y", here's the working plan.

## Undergraduate timeline (via UCAS)

### Y-2 to Y-1 — Foundation phase (12+ months before)

**~24 months before:**
- Decide UK is the target country.
- Begin saving / planning family finances.
- Identify 2-3 subjects you're seriously considering.

**~18 months before (around January of Y-1 / Class 11):**
- Start IELTS preparation. Aim to take the test by June Y-1.
- Research universities. Build a shortlist by subject ranking, location, cost.
- Read recent course syllabi for your top universities.

### Y-1 — Application year

**June Y-1 (immediately after +2 finals):**
- Take **IELTS for UKVI** — book at British Council Nepal — https://www.britishcouncil.org.np/exam/ielts.
- Aim for IELTS 6.5 overall (7.0 if Oxbridge/medicine target).
- Begin first draft of **personal statement**.
- Confirm finances — talk to bank about education loan if needed.

**July Y-1:**
- Finalise university shortlist (up to **5 choices via UCAS**).
- Get **academic references** lined up — give referees 4-6 weeks notice.
- Polish personal statement. Show to 2-3 people for feedback.
- Get NEB transcripts in English; start MoEST + MoFA attestation chain.

**August–September Y-1:**
- **UCAS application opens** (typically early September).
- Submit application early — universities review on a **rolling basis**, even before the official equal-consideration deadline.

**October Y-1:**
- **15 October Y-1 deadline:** Oxford, Cambridge, medicine, dentistry, veterinary [Source: UCAS deadlines — https://www.ucas.com/undergraduate/applying-university/when-apply].
- Most other applications should also be submitted by **late October** — earlier = better chance for popular courses.

**November–December Y-1:**
- Sit any **admissions tests** (LNAT for law, BMAT/UCAT for medicine, etc.).
- Attend any university interviews (usually for Oxbridge, medicine, top art schools).
- Some universities issue offers (conditional or unconditional) from mid-October.

**January Y-1 / Y (transition):**
- **~29 January Y-1 deadline** (verify current year at https://www.ucas.com/) — equal-consideration deadline for most UK undergraduate courses.

### Y — Decision and visa year

**February–April Y:**
- Continue to receive offers.
- Reply to offers via UCAS — choose **Firm (first choice)** and **Insurance (backup)** by the UCAS deadline (typically early May).

**May Y:**
- Receive final NEB Grade 12 transcript / certificate.
- Submit final transcripts to your firm offer university.
- Confirm conditions of offer are met.

**June Y:**
- Pay tuition deposit if required.
- Receive **CAS** from the university.
- **Apply for the Student visa** — earliest is 6 months before course start (so for September Y, earliest is March Y, but realistic is June–July Y).
- **Book biometrics** at VFS Kathmandu — https://visa.vfsglobal.com/npl/en/gbr.
- Take **TB test** at IOM Nepal — https://nepal.iom.int/.
- Ensure **maintenance funds** are in the account for full 28 days before applying.

**July Y:**
- Submit visa application.
- Attend biometrics + any credibility interview.
- Receive visa decision (usually within 3 weeks; can be 4–6 weeks in peak).

**August Y:**
- Book flight to UK.
- Arrange accommodation (university halls or private rented). Most universities open accommodation applications in spring; book before late June for guaranteed allocation.
- Buy travel insurance.
- Notify your bank in Nepal of upcoming international transactions.

**September Y — arrival**

- Arrive 1-2 weeks before the course starts.
- Collect BRP / check eVisa.
- Enrol at the university.
- Set up bank account, GP, transport pass.
- Start the course.

## Postgraduate (master's) timeline — direct application

Master's applications are **rolling** — no single deadline; you apply directly to each university. But popular programmes (Oxbridge, LSE, UCL, top business schools) close their first deadlines in late October or December of the previous year.

### Y-1

**June–August Y-1:**
- Take IELTS / TOEFL / GMAT / GRE as required.
- Begin SOP drafts (per university).
- Request academic + professional references.
- Research scholarships (deadlines often Aug–Nov for next academic year).

**September Y-1:**
- Most universities open postgraduate applications.
- Submit Oxbridge / LSE / Imperial / top US-style early decision applications.

**October Y-1:**
- **Chevening Scholarship deadline** (typically early November). Apply if eligible — https://www.chevening.org/.
- Submit most postgraduate applications.

**November–December Y-1:**
- **Commonwealth Scholarship** application window (typically open October, close December) — https://cscuk.fcdo.gov.uk/.
- Universities begin issuing offers.

**January–March Y:**
- Receive most offers.
- Decide. Pay tuition deposit.
- Begin visa preparation.

**April–July Y:**
- Receive CAS.
- Apply for Student visa.
- Take TB test, prepare finances.

**August–September Y:**
- Fly to UK, enrol, start master's.

## January intake

A smaller intake offered by ~30% of UK universities. Useful for:
- Students who missed the September UCAS deadline.
- Students who took longer to prepare IELTS or finances.
- Some master's programmes.

**Timeline:** Apply June–September of the previous year, visa decision November–December, arrive late December / early January.

Fewer universities, fewer scholarships, fewer cohort-mate options. But valid.

## Common timeline mistakes

1. **Booking IELTS too late.** Score reports take 13 days; if your January deadline is 29th, taking IELTS on the 20th means you might miss it.
2. **Starting attestation in July.** MoEST + MoFA take 1-2 weeks each; start by May at the latest.
3. **Waiting for IELTS results before starting university applications.** Most universities accept applications with "IELTS pending" — submit and update later.
4. **Applying for visa before 28-day fund-holding window.** If your bank statement window doesn't end within 31 days of visa application, you're refused.
5. **Booking flights before visa is granted.** Refundable flights or wait until visa approval.

## Hard deadlines summary

| Event | Hard deadline |
|---|---|
| Oxbridge / medicine UCAS | 15 October (year before entry) |
| UCAS equal consideration (most courses) | ~29 January (year of entry) — verify at https://www.ucas.com/ |
| UCAS final / Clearing | Late June (year of entry) |
| Chevening Scholarship | Early November (year before entry) |
| Commonwealth Scholarship | December (year before entry) — verify https://cscuk.fcdo.gov.uk/ |
| Visa application earliest | 6 months before course start |
| Visa application latest | Realistically 4 weeks before course start (3-week processing + buffer) |
| TB test | Done in the 6 months before visa application |
| Funds in account (28 days) | Window must end within 31 days of visa application |
