# UK Application — Master Document Checklist for Nepali Students

Two document phases: (A) **university application** documents, and (B) **visa application** documents. Many overlap, but they go to different recipients with different formats. Below is the complete checklist with the Nepal-specific attestation flow.

## Phase A — University application (UCAS or direct)

### A1. Personal information

- Valid **passport** — must be valid for the full course duration plus 6 months. Renew at the Department of Passports Nepal before applying if it's close to expiry.

### A2. Academic transcripts

- **NEB / HSEB +2 marksheet and character certificate** — original and certified true copy.
- **SLC / SEE certificate** — for some universities (especially Oxbridge).
- **Bachelor's transcripts** (for master's applicants) — semester-wise transcripts, character certificate, provisional certificate (if degree pending), and final degree certificate.
- All in **English** — if originally in Nepali, translated by an authorised translator and stamped.

**Attestation flow for international use:**
1. Issued by school / university registrar.
2. Verified by **Ministry of Education, Science and Technology (MoEST), Nepal** — Singha Durbar, Kathmandu.
3. Apostilled / attested by **Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MoFA), Nepal** — Tripureshwor, Kathmandu.

This MoEST + MoFA two-stamp chain is what makes documents internationally accepted. Allow **1–2 weeks** in Nepal for both steps.

### A3. English language test

- **IELTS / TOEFL / PTE** report — see file 03 for SELT requirements.
- Take **IELTS for UKVI** specifically (not regular IELTS) if it's for both university and visa.

### A4. Personal statement / Statement of Purpose

- **Undergraduate (UCAS):** one 4,000-character / 47-line personal statement — submitted via UCAS, sent to up to 5 universities.
- **Postgraduate:** university-specific. Usually 500–1,000 words per university.
- Should cover: **why this course, why this university, what relevant academic + extracurricular experience you have, what your career goal is**. Specific to the university — generic copies are detected and rejected.

### A5. References / Letters of Recommendation

- **Undergraduate:** usually **one academic reference** via UCAS — from your +2 college, principal or subject teacher.
- **Postgraduate:** usually **two academic references** (or one academic + one professional if you've worked) from people who've supervised your work.

References should be on **institutional letterhead**, signed, with contact details. Universities may verify with the referee directly.

### A6. CV / Résumé (for postgraduate)

1–2 pages, focused on academic + relevant professional + research/project experience.

### A7. Portfolio (for art, design, architecture, journalism)

University-specific format. Usually uploaded via SlideRoom or the university's own portal.

### A8. Optional but useful

- **Scholarship application materials** — usually a separate essay.
- **Research proposal** — for PhD applications, 1,500–3,000 words.
- **Internship certificates** — if mentioned in SOP.

### A9. Application fee

- **Undergraduate via UCAS:** £28.50 for a single choice, £28.50 for up to 5 choices (2024/25 — verify at https://www.ucas.com/).
- **Postgraduate (direct application):** £25–£75 per university; some waive fees for international applicants.

## Phase B — Visa application (after CAS issued)

### B1. Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS)

Issued by your university free of charge after you've:
- Met all conditions of your offer (academic + English).
- Paid your tuition deposit (if required by the university).
- Provided required ID and bank documents.

The CAS contains a unique reference number (starts with letter codes) — you enter this when applying for the visa.

### B2. Passport

- Same passport you used for the CAS.
- Must be valid for full visa length.

### B3. Financial evidence

See file 04 for the rules. Provide one of:
- **Bank statement** (28-day rule, see file 04).
- **Bank certificate / balance letter** on letterhead.
- **Loan letter** from a regulated Nepali bank.

If the funds are in your parent's account:
- Parent's bank document.
- **Parental consent letter** — original signature.
- **Your birth certificate** showing the parent-child relationship (if your passport doesn't already show parent names).

### B4. English language evidence

- **IELTS for UKVI** test result (or other approved SELT) — see file 03.
- The test must be **within 2 years of the visa application** [Source: GOV.UK SELT — https://www.gov.uk/guidance/prove-your-english-language-abilities-with-a-secure-english-language-test-selt].

### B5. TB test certificate

- Must be done at an **IOM-approved clinic in Nepal** — full list at https://nepal.iom.int/.
- Valid for **6 months** from the date of the test [Source: GOV.UK TB test — https://www.gov.uk/tb-test-visa].
- Cost approximately NPR 7,500–9,000.

### B6. Academic qualifications

- Same transcripts / certificates listed on your CAS.
- Bring **originals** to the visa application appointment if requested.

### B7. ATAS certificate (if applicable)

Required for some postgraduate research and taught programmes in sensitive subjects (engineering, applied maths, physics, chemistry, computing) at master's and PhD level [Source: GOV.UK ATAS — https://www.gov.uk/guidance/academic-technology-approval-scheme]. Free, takes 30+ days.

Your CAS will state if you need ATAS.

### B8. Parental consent (if under 18)

- Notarised consent letter.
- Both parents' signatures.
- Birth certificate.
- Details of guardian / care arrangements in the UK.

## Phase C — On arrival in the UK

### C1. Within 10 days of arrival (or by visa expiry, whichever sooner)

- Collect your **Biometric Residence Permit (BRP)** from the post office named on your visa decision letter (or check your eVisa account if you have an eVisa). [Source: GOV.UK BRP — https://www.gov.uk/biometric-residence-permits/collect]
- Note: the UK is transitioning from BRP to **eVisa**; check https://www.gov.uk/evisa for your specific status.

### C2. University enrolment

- Bring your **CAS letter, passport, BRP/eVisa, original academic documents, original IELTS report**.
- Enrolment is mandatory — your visa is conditional on enrolment.

### C3. Open a UK bank account

- Most banks (HSBC, Barclays, NatWest, Lloyds, Monzo, Starling) offer free student accounts.
- Bring: **passport, BRP/eVisa, university letter confirming enrolment with your UK address, proof of UK address (utility bill, tenancy agreement)**.

### C4. Register with a GP (general practitioner)

Free under NHS once you've paid the IHS. Find a local GP at https://www.nhs.uk/service-search/find-a-gp.

### C5. Get a National Insurance number

If you intend to work — apply at https://www.gov.uk/apply-national-insurance-number.

### C6. Get a Student Oyster card (London) or rail/bus pass

- **London 18+ Student Oyster Photocard** — 30% off Travelcards and Bus & Tram Passes [Source: TfL — https://tfl.gov.uk/fares/free-and-discounted-travel/18-plus-student-oyster-photocard].
- **16-25 / 26-30 Railcard** — 1/3 off most train fares.

## Common document mistakes

1. **Name mismatch.** Passport says "Anita Kumari Sharma" but transcripts say "Anita Sharma". Get an affidavit from MoFA noting the names refer to the same person.
2. **Unsigned bank statements.** Bank document MUST be on letterhead and signed/stamped.
3. **Expired IELTS / TOEFL.** Valid for 2 years from the test date.
4. **Wrong TB test centre.** Only IOM-approved clinics; the test from a regular clinic is rejected.
5. **Missing MoFA apostille.** Some universities/visa officers reject un-apostilled transcripts.
6. **CAS issued for the wrong course.** Verify everything on the CAS matches what you applied for; ask university to reissue if incorrect — they will.
