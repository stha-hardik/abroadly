# Post-Study Routes from a UK Student Visa

After your course finishes you have two main legitimate paths to stay and work in the UK: the **Graduate Route (Graduate visa)** and switching to a **Skilled Worker visa** sponsored by an employer.

## Graduate visa (the "post-study work" visa)

[Source: GOV.UK Graduate visa — https://www.gov.uk/graduate-visa]

The Graduate Route is the simplest post-study option — you can work in **any job, at any skill level, for any employer (or be self-employed)** without employer sponsorship.

### Eligibility

- You must currently hold a **Student visa** (or have one ending) [Source: GOV.UK Graduate visa — https://www.gov.uk/graduate-visa].
- You must have **completed an eligible course** — a UK bachelor's, master's, or PhD from a sponsor with **Track Record status**.
- You must **apply before your Student visa expires** and be inside the UK at the time of application.
- Your university must confirm you've completed the course; you usually wait for them to report this to the Home Office before applying.

### Duration

- **Bachelor's or master's degree:** **2 years** if you apply on or before **31 December 2026**; **18 months** if you apply on or after **1 January 2027** [Source: GOV.UK Graduate visa — https://www.gov.uk/graduate-visa].
- **PhD:** **3 years**.

This is a one-time visa — **you cannot extend the Graduate Route or apply for it again**. After it expires you must leave the UK or switch to another visa class.

### Cost (2026)

- Application fee: **£937** [Source: GOV.UK Graduate visa — https://www.gov.uk/graduate-visa].
- Immigration Health Surcharge: **~£1,035 per year** [Source: GOV.UK Graduate visa — https://www.gov.uk/graduate-visa]. So 2 years = ~£2,070 IHS + £937 = ~£3,007 total (verify with current calculator).

### What you can do

- Work in **any job** (most jobs — exclusions below).
- Be **self-employed**, freelance, or start a business.
- Travel in and out of the UK freely.
- Switch to a different visa from within the UK.

### What you can't do

- Work as a professional sportsperson [Source: GOV.UK Graduate visa — https://www.gov.uk/graduate-visa].
- Apply for most public benefits.
- Bring new dependents (existing dependents on your Student visa can extend with you; new partners/children typically cannot be added).
- Extend the Graduate visa itself.

### When to apply

Apply **before** your Student visa expires. Recommended: apply 1–2 months before expiry, after your university has reported your course completion to UKVI.

## Switching to a Skilled Worker visa

The Skilled Worker visa is the main long-term work route in the UK. It leads to settlement (ILR) after 5 years.

### Eligibility (2026)

[Source: GOV.UK Skilled Worker visa — https://www.gov.uk/skilled-worker-visa]

- A **job offer from a UK employer with a Sponsor Licence** for the Skilled Worker route.
- The job must be on the **eligible occupations list** (most roles at RQF level 3 and above).
- **Minimum salary:** Generally **£38,700/year or the occupation-specific "going rate," whichever is higher**, as of April 2024 thresholds [Source: GOV.UK Skilled Worker salary — https://www.gov.uk/skilled-worker-visa/your-job]. New-entrant rates and shortage-occupation discounts may apply for recent graduates — check the latest rules.
- English at B1 level (typically already met if you've completed a UK degree).
- Sufficient maintenance funds (usually £1,270, often waived if employer attests).

### Cost

- Application fee from inside the UK (switching): £719–£885 depending on length [Source: GOV.UK Skilled Worker fees — https://www.gov.uk/skilled-worker-visa].
- Immigration Skills Charge: paid by employer (£1,000 per year, large employers).
- IHS: £1,035 per year.
- Visa length: 1 to 5 years initially; renewable; leads to ILR after 5 years.

### Strategy: Graduate Route → Skilled Worker

The common path for a Nepali student:

1. Finish bachelor's or master's.
2. Apply for the Graduate Route → get 2 years of free job-search and work.
3. Within those 2 years, secure a Skilled Worker sponsor.
4. Switch from Graduate to Skilled Worker in-country.
5. After 5 years on Skilled Worker, apply for ILR (Indefinite Leave to Remain) — permanent residency.
6. After 12 months on ILR, apply for British citizenship.

**Total time to citizenship from arriving on a Student visa: ~9–11 years.**

### Risk: shrinking Graduate Route + rising Skilled Worker salary

The Graduate Route window narrows to 18 months from 1 January 2027. Skilled Worker salary thresholds have risen sharply since 2024 — many entry-level graduate roles are below the threshold. Net effect: harder to make the Student → Graduate → Skilled Worker chain work for non-STEM, non-finance careers. Plan accordingly.

## Other less-common routes

### Innovator Founder visa

For graduates with a genuine, innovative business idea endorsed by an approved body. Self-employment + business-founding route. Requires substantive endorsement and investment funds. [Source: GOV.UK Innovator Founder visa — https://www.gov.uk/innovator-founder-visa]

### Global Talent visa

For exceptional talent in academia, science, engineering, tech, arts. High bar; usually requires existing track record. [Source: GOV.UK Global Talent visa — https://www.gov.uk/global-talent]

### High Potential Individual visa

For graduates of certain top global universities (list on GOV.UK). 2 years (or 3 for PhD), unsponsored. Nepal-graduates are not eligible based on Nepali degrees alone, but a UK graduate could use a previous degree if it's from a listed institution. [Source: GOV.UK High Potential Individual visa — https://www.gov.uk/high-potential-individual-visa]

### Family / partner visa

If you marry a British citizen or settled person during your studies, you can switch to a partner visa. Specific income / cohabitation requirements apply.

## Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR) and citizenship

[Source: GOV.UK Indefinite Leave to Remain — https://www.gov.uk/indefinite-leave-to-remain]

- 5 continuous years on Skilled Worker (or other settlement-eligible route) → eligible for ILR.
- Time on **Student visa and Graduate visa do not count** toward the 5 years for ILR.
- Pass Life in the UK test and English language requirement.
- After 12 months on ILR → eligible to apply for naturalisation as a British citizen [Source: GOV.UK Apply for citizenship — https://www.gov.uk/apply-citizenship-indefinite-leave-to-remain].

## If you want to return to Nepal

The UK does not require you to stay. Returning to Nepal after your studies (with or without using the Graduate Route) is fully legitimate and often the best career move depending on your sector — particularly in development, healthcare, government, or family business contexts.
