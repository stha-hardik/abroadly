# UK Entry Requirements for Nepali Students

There are two parts to admission: **academic** (what the university wants to see on your transcripts) and **English-language** (what UKVI and the university require). Both must be met or your CAS won't be issued.

## Academic requirements — undergraduate

UK universities use UCAS Tariff points, but they also publish direct equivalencies for international qualifications.

| Nepali qualification | Typical UK university expectation |
|---|---|
| NEB +2 (Class 12) | Direct entry **to most universities** with 70–80% aggregate (3.0–3.5 GPA) [Source: UCAS — https://www.ucas.com/]. For Russell Group / Oxbridge, typically 85%+ (3.6 GPA+) and frequently an A-Level top-up or foundation year is required [Source: University of Cambridge international qualifications — https://www.undergraduate.study.cam.ac.uk/applying/international-qualifications]. |
| A-Levels (3 subjects) | Direct entry. Russell Group typically asks AAA–AAB. |
| HSEB / TU "Intermediate" | Direct entry to most universities; some require a foundation year. |
| Cambridge International A-Levels from a Nepali school | Treated identically to UK A-Levels. |
| Diploma / Pre-University from CIT/CTEVT | Foundation year usually required before bachelor's. |

> **GAP:** Every UK university publishes its own "International Qualifications" page with country-specific minimums for Nepal. The chart above is indicative — for a binding requirement, always check the destination university's official page (e.g., University of Manchester international students — https://www.manchester.ac.uk/study/international/admissions/country/nepal/).

### Foundation year (if you don't meet direct entry)

A 1-year foundation programme inside the UK that leads into year 1 of a bachelor's. Run either by the university itself or by external pathway providers (Kaplan, INTO, Study Group). Cost is comparable to one year of university tuition (£15,000–£22,000). Useful when your aggregate is borderline or your subjects don't match the target degree.

## Academic requirements — postgraduate

A bachelor's degree from a Nepali university recognised by UGC Nepal — typically with **2:1 equivalent (60%+ / 3.2 GPA+)** for most master's programmes, **2:2 equivalent (50%+ / 2.8 GPA+)** for less competitive programmes [Source: UK NARIC / Ecctis country profile — https://www.ecctis.com/].

Top universities (Oxbridge, Imperial, LSE, UCL) frequently require **first-class equivalent (70%+ / 3.7 GPA+)** plus a strong personal statement.

## English-language requirements

UKVI accepts a specific list of "Secure English Language Tests" (SELT) for visa purposes. Most universities also accept others for admission alone, but for the **visa application** the test must be on the SELT list [Source: GOV.UK Approved Secure English Language Tests — https://www.gov.uk/guidance/prove-your-english-language-abilities-with-a-secure-english-language-test-selt].

### SELT-approved tests (Nepal)

- **IELTS for UKVI Academic** — usually the default. Must be taken at an authorised IELTS for UKVI test centre, not a regular IELTS centre [Source: IELTS UKVI — https://www.ielts.org/take-a-test/test-types/ielts-for-ukvi].
- **Pearson PTE Academic UKVI** — accepted from late 2020 onwards.
- **LanguageCert International ESOL SELT** — accepted.

### Typical minimum scores

| Course level | IELTS overall | Per-component minimum |
|---|---|---|
| Foundation / pre-sessional | 4.5–5.5 | 4.5 in each |
| Undergraduate | 6.0–6.5 | 5.5–6.0 in each |
| Postgraduate (taught) | 6.5 | 6.0 in each |
| Postgraduate (research / Russell Group) | 7.0 | 6.5+ in each |
| Medicine, dentistry, law, journalism | 7.0–7.5 | 7.0 in each |

> **GAP:** These are typical floors. The binding number is the **specific course page** at the university. Never rely on a general "for UK universities" figure — courses like Cambridge's MPhil often require IELTS 7.5 overall with no element below 7.0 [Source: Cambridge English language requirements — https://www.postgraduate.study.cam.ac.uk/international/competence-english].

### IELTS for UKVI vs regular IELTS

For your **visa**, you must take **IELTS for UKVI** — not regular IELTS. Same exam content, but the test centre is UKVI-approved and the score report has a unique reference UKVI accepts. Some universities will accept a regular IELTS for **admission** but UKVI will not accept it for the **visa**. Booking via British Council Nepal — https://www.britishcouncil.org.np/exam/ielts.

### IELTS waivers

Possible if your prior qualification was taught entirely in English and the institution is on the UKVI list of accredited Majority English-speaking countries. Nepali medium of instruction generally does **not** qualify for a waiver — confirm with the specific university; do not assume.

## Document equivalency

Nepal's NEB Grade 12 transcript may need to be assessed by **Ecctis (formerly UK NARIC)** for some universities — https://www.ecctis.com/. Many universities accept the transcript directly without an Ecctis statement; check the specific institution.
