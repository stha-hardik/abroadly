# UK Cities Where Nepali Students Study

Choosing a city changes your total cost by tens of thousands of pounds across a degree, and changes job prospects after. Below are the most common UK cities for Nepali students with neutral cost-of-living and employability information from official sources.

## London

- **Cost positioning:** Highest in the UK. UKVI's official maintenance proof is **£1,529 per month inside London** vs £1,171 outside [Source: GOV.UK Student visa: money — https://www.gov.uk/student-visa/money]. Real rent for a student room is £900–£1,500/month in zones 2–4; food and transport push the realistic total to £1,800–£2,500/month.
- **Transport:** Excellent. Single Tube journey via 18+ Student Oyster Card discount; monthly travelcard inside zones 1–2 around £150 [Source: Transport for London — https://tfl.gov.uk/fares/].
- **Universities:** Imperial, UCL, LSE, King's College London, Queen Mary, Goldsmiths, City, University of London, SOAS, Brunel, Westminster, Greenwich, East London, Middlesex, Roehampton, plus many specialist colleges.
- **Job market:** Largest international job market in the UK. Strong for finance, tech, consulting, media, NGOs. Higher entry-level wages than rest of UK.
- **Trade-off:** Highest cost. Easy to overspend. Long commutes from affordable areas.
- **Best for:** Students whose course requires London (e.g., finance/law/media specialisms), or who have a strong scholarship that offsets the cost.

## Manchester

- **Cost:** Lower than London by ~30%. Realistic monthly £1,200–£1,600.
- **Transport:** Decent. Metrolink tram, buses, some areas walkable.
- **Universities:** University of Manchester (Russell Group), Manchester Metropolitan, Salford, Royal Northern College of Music.
- **Job market:** Strong for media (BBC Salford), engineering, healthcare, fintech. UK's second city by many measures.
- **Best for:** Students who want a major-city experience without London prices. Large Nepali community.

## Birmingham

- **Cost:** ~30% cheaper than London. £1,100–£1,500/month realistic.
- **Transport:** Buses + trains; new Metro line; many areas walkable.
- **Universities:** University of Birmingham (Russell Group), Aston, Birmingham City, University College Birmingham.
- **Job market:** Strong for engineering, automotive (Jaguar Land Rover area), business services, healthcare.
- **Best for:** STEM students; well-served by direct flights from India (Delhi connection often cheaper than London).

## Edinburgh (Scotland)

- **Cost:** Mid-range. £1,200–£1,700/month realistic.
- **Transport:** Walkable city centre; trams + buses.
- **Universities:** University of Edinburgh (Russell Group, ancient), Heriot-Watt, Edinburgh Napier, Queen Margaret.
- **Note:** Scottish bachelor's degrees are **4 years**, not 3 [Source: UCAS — https://www.ucas.com/]. Master's are the same 1-year length as England.
- **Job market:** Strong in finance, tech, life sciences, tourism, civil service.
- **Best for:** Students who specifically want Edinburgh University or want a smaller, walkable historic city.

## Glasgow (Scotland)

- **Cost:** Lower than Edinburgh. £1,000–£1,400/month realistic.
- **Universities:** University of Glasgow (Russell Group), Strathclyde, Glasgow Caledonian, Royal Conservatoire.
- **Best for:** Engineering at Strathclyde, business/economics at Glasgow. Strong industrial heritage.

## Coventry

- **Cost:** Among the cheapest student cities. £950–£1,300/month realistic.
- **Universities:** Coventry University, University of Warwick (Russell Group, technically in Coventry's outskirts), Birmingham 25 min away by train.
- **Best for:** Cost-conscious students; Warwick is one of the strongest non-London Russell Group options for business, economics, computer science.

## Sheffield

- **Cost:** Low. £1,000–£1,400/month realistic.
- **Universities:** University of Sheffield (Russell Group), Sheffield Hallam.
- **Best for:** Engineering, materials science, computer science. Strong Nepali community.

## Newcastle / Gateshead

- **Cost:** Low. £950–£1,350/month realistic.
- **Universities:** Newcastle University (Russell Group), Northumbria, Sunderland.
- **Best for:** Affordable Russell Group education; growing tech sector.

## Liverpool

- **Cost:** Low to mid. £1,000–£1,400/month realistic.
- **Universities:** University of Liverpool (Russell Group), Liverpool John Moores, Liverpool Hope.
- **Best for:** Affordable big-city life; strong in medicine, veterinary, maritime.

## Cardiff (Wales)

- **Cost:** Mid. £1,100–£1,500/month realistic.
- **Universities:** Cardiff University (Russell Group), Cardiff Metropolitan, University of South Wales.
- **Note:** Wales has separate devolved education policy but UKVI visa rules are identical to England.
- **Best for:** Journalism, dentistry, business.

## Bristol

- **Cost:** Higher than other non-London cities. £1,200–£1,700/month realistic.
- **Universities:** University of Bristol (Russell Group), UWE Bristol.
- **Best for:** Aerospace, engineering, creative industries. Closer to London than Manchester.

## Cities to consider more carefully

- **Smaller towns** (Lincoln, Falmouth, Carlisle, Hull) — often very affordable but with limited internship/job markets. Fine for the degree itself, harder for the Graduate Route afterwards.
- **Belfast, Northern Ireland** — Queen's University Belfast is Russell Group with low cost-of-living; smaller Nepali community.

## Safety

UK is generally safe for international students. As with any country: avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas; register with your university's wellbeing service in week 1; keep your BRP / eVisa accessible. For travel advisories specific to Nepal-UK movement, check the Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office at https://www.gov.uk/foreign-travel-advice and the Embassy of Nepal in London at https://uk.nepalembassy.gov.np/.
