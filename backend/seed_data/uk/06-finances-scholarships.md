# UK Finances and Scholarships for Nepali Students

## Total annual budget

For visa purposes, the minimum proof is **tuition + 9 months living cost**. Realistic actual cost is higher — most Nepali students underestimate winter clothing, transport, and admin fees.

### Tuition (international, indicative 2025/26)

| Programme type | London | Outside London |
|---|---|---|
| Foundation year | £15,000–£22,000 | £12,000–£18,000 |
| Bachelor's (arts/humanities) | £18,000–£28,000 | £15,000–£22,000 |
| Bachelor's (sciences/engineering) | £25,000–£36,000 | £18,000–£28,000 |
| Bachelor's (medicine/dentistry) | £35,000–£60,000 | £30,000–£55,000 |
| Master's (taught, most subjects) | £20,000–£35,000 | £15,000–£28,000 |
| Master's (MBA) | £30,000–£100,000+ | £20,000–£60,000 |

> **GAP:** These are typical ranges. The binding figure is the university's official "International tuition" page for your specific course and academic year. Always verify there.

Major cost categories: accommodation (50–60%), food (15%), transport (5–10%), books and supplies (5%), phone and internet (3%), social and clothing (10–15%).

## Funding sources Nepali students actually use

### 1. Self / family funding

Most common. Requires proof per file 04. Family income source must be plausibly large enough to cover 1 year of tuition + living comfortably.

### 2. Education loan from a Nepali bank

Available from most commercial banks. Indicative terms — **always verify with the bank directly**:

- **NIC Asia Bank** — Education Loan up to NPR 1 crore, requires collateral usually (real estate). Interest rate typically 9–13% [Source: NIC Asia Education Loan — https://www.nicasiabank.com/loans/education-loan/].
- **Nabil Bank** — Education Loan with similar collateral and rate structure [Source: Nabil Bank — https://www.nabilbank.com/personal/loans/education-loan].
- **Sanima Bank** — https://www.sanimabank.com/loan/education-loan.
- **Standard Chartered Nepal** — for select profile, generally to top universities.

For visa purposes, the loan letter must:
- Be on bank letterhead, signed and stamped.
- State the loan is sanctioned (not just approved in principle).
- State the amount and currency.
- State the funds are available for study in the UK.
- Be issued within 6 months of your visa application.

### 3. Major scholarships open to Nepali nationals

Below are scholarships explicitly open to Nepal. Always verify eligibility and deadlines at the official URL.

#### Chevening Scholarship (UK government, for postgraduate)
- Eligibility: Nepal is on the eligible-countries list [Source: Chevening — https://www.chevening.org/scholarship/eligibility/].
- Covers full tuition, monthly stipend, return economy flights, visa fee, IHS, arrival allowance.
- For 1-year master's only.
- **Requires:** undergraduate degree, **2 years (2,800 hours) of work experience**, English ability, return to Nepal for at least 2 years after the scholarship ends.
- Applications open **early August**, close **early November** each year for the following September intake [Source: Chevening — https://www.chevening.org/].
- Around 7–12 awards per year for Nepal (varies). Highly competitive.

#### Commonwealth Scholarships (UK, for postgraduate)
- Eligibility: Nepal is a Commonwealth country [Source: Commonwealth Scholarship Commission — https://cscuk.fcdo.gov.uk/].
- Several streams: master's, PhD, distance-learning, professional fellowships.
- Covers tuition, stipend, flights, study/research support grant.
- Applications typically open October, close December each year.
- Nominated through Nepal's Ministry of Education for some streams; direct application for others.

#### GREAT Scholarships (British Council + UK universities)
- Eligibility: Nepal is on the targeted countries list [Source: British Council GREAT Scholarships — https://study-uk.britishcouncil.org/scholarships/great].
- ~£10,000 toward tuition for a one-year master's.
- Each participating university runs its own selection — apply through the university directly.

#### University-specific international scholarships
- **Oxford** — Reach Oxford, Clarendon (postgraduate) — https://www.ox.ac.uk/admissions/graduate/fees-and-funding/fees-funding-and-scholarship-search.
- **Cambridge** — Cambridge International Scholarship, Gates Cambridge (US-funded but open globally) — https://www.cambridgetrust.org/scholarships/.
- **LSE** — Graduate Support Scheme — https://www.lse.ac.uk/study-at-lse/Graduate/Fees-and-Funding/Financial-Support.
- **Edinburgh** — Edinburgh Global Master's Scholarships — https://www.ed.ac.uk/student-funding/postgraduate.
- **Manchester** — Manchester Master's Bursary, Equity & Merit Scholarships — https://www.manchester.ac.uk/study/international/finance-and-scholarships/.
- **Warwick** — Chancellor's International Scholarship — https://warwick.ac.uk/services/dc/schols_fund/.

Always check each university's "International scholarships" page directly — the URLs and amounts change annually.

### 4. Department / subject scholarships

Many UK universities offer **subject-specific bursaries** (£3,000–£10,000) for international students with strong academics. These are buried inside each department's page. Apply early — most close in March–May for September intake.

## Cost-saving strategies

- **Apply to universities outside London.** Maintenance proof drops by £4,300+ per year, real living cost drops further.
- **Pay tuition in full upfront if you can.** Many universities offer a 5–10% discount.
- **Choose universities that include winter accommodation in the academic year contract** (most do; some don't — you'd pay extra for Christmas + Easter).
- **Take a part-time job.** You can work up to 20 hours per week during term, 40 hours during vacations on a Student visa [Source: GOV.UK Student visa work — https://www.gov.uk/student-visa/work]. Typical wage £11–£14 per hour.
- **Use NHS, free public libraries, student discount cards** (NUS / TOTUM, Railcards, ISIC).
