# UK Student Visa — For Applicants from Nepal

The visa class is called the **Student visa** (formerly Tier 4 General). You apply online directly on GOV.UK and submit biometrics at a Visa Application Centre (VAC) in Nepal.

**Authoritative pages — always check these for current rules:**
- Overview: https://www.gov.uk/student-visa
- Apply from outside the UK: https://www.gov.uk/student-visa/apply
- Maintenance funds: https://www.gov.uk/student-visa/money
- Documents you must provide: https://www.gov.uk/student-visa/documents-you-must-provide

## Eligibility checklist

You can apply if all of the below are true [Source: GOV.UK Student visa — https://www.gov.uk/student-visa]:

1. You've been offered a place on a course by a UK higher-education provider with a **Student Sponsor Licence (Track Record status)** — verify the provider at https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/register-of-licensed-sponsors-students.
2. You have a valid **Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS)** issued by that sponsor.
3. You can prove **financial maintenance** at the levels below.
4. You can prove your **English language ability** with a SELT (see file 03).
5. You have a valid passport, biometric residence permit photos, and a TB test certificate from an IOM-approved clinic in Nepal — https://nepal.iom.int/.
6. If you're under 18, parental consent letter.

## Maintenance funds (the part that gets people refused)

You must show **course tuition (the first year) PLUS living costs**. Living-cost figures from GOV.UK:

- **London (or studying primarily inside Greater London):** £1,529 per month [Source: GOV.UK Student visa: money — https://www.gov.uk/student-visa/money]
- **Outside London:** £1,171 per month [Source: GOV.UK Student visa: money — https://www.gov.uk/student-visa/money]
- **Maximum:** 9 months (so totals are £13,761 for London, £10,539 for outside London) [Source: GOV.UK Student visa: money — https://www.gov.uk/student-visa/money]

Plus **first year of tuition fees** as stated on your CAS, minus any deposit you've already paid (the CAS will show this).

### How long the money must sit

The maintenance funds must have been in the account for **at least 28 consecutive days, ending within 31 days of the date you apply** [Source: GOV.UK Student visa: money — https://www.gov.uk/student-visa/money]. So if you apply on 1 August, the 28-day window must end no earlier than 1 July, and the account must have shown the full amount on every day of that window.

### Whose account

Your own account, your parent's account (with a signed parental consent letter + birth certificate proving the relationship), or a regulated financial institution loan letter [Source: GOV.UK Student visa: money — https://www.gov.uk/student-visa/money]. **Friends or other relatives are not allowed.** Joint accounts allowed only if the joint holder is you or your parent.

### Acceptable financial evidence

- Bank statement, dated within 31 days of application, on bank letterhead, signed and stamped.
- Bank certificate / balance certificate on letterhead.
- Educational loan letter from a regulated bank in Nepal (NIC Asia, Nabil, Standard Chartered, etc.) — must say the loan is in your name, the amount, and that it's available for study in the UK.
- Pension fund / fixed deposit statements — accepted in many cases; verify with the embassy.

**Not accepted:** mobile-money wallet balances, cryptocurrency, third-party guarantor letters from non-parents.

## Documents you must submit

[Source: GOV.UK — Documents you must provide — https://www.gov.uk/student-visa/documents-you-must-provide]

1. Current passport (with a blank page).
2. CAS reference number (from your university — they upload your file).
3. Proof of finances (see above).
4. Proof of English language ability (SELT report).
5. Tuberculosis (TB) test certificate from an **IOM-approved clinic in Nepal** — required because Nepal is on the UK TB screening list [Source: GOV.UK TB test for visa — https://www.gov.uk/tb-test-visa].
6. ATAS certificate if your subject is sensitive (most engineering, physics, applied maths PhDs; some master's) — check at https://www.gov.uk/guidance/academic-technology-approval-scheme.
7. Parental consent and birth certificate if you're under 18.
8. Academic transcripts and qualification certificates listed on your CAS.

## Timing

- **Earliest you can apply** from outside the UK: 6 months before your course start date [Source: GOV.UK Student visa — https://www.gov.uk/student-visa].
- **Typical decision time:** within 3 weeks of biometrics submission [Source: GOV.UK Student visa — https://www.gov.uk/student-visa]. June–September peak: often 4–6 weeks. Apply early.
- **Priority service:** decision in 5 working days; **Super-Priority:** decision next working day. Both cost extra.

## Common refusal reasons (Nepal-specific patterns)

1. **Funds not held for full 28 days.** A balance drop of even one day inside the 28-day window invalidates the proof.
2. **CAS issued for a different course / different tuition fee.** The numbers on your application must match the CAS exactly.
3. **English test taken at a non-UKVI centre.** Easy mistake — book IELTS for UKVI specifically.
4. **TB certificate from a non-IOM clinic.** Use only the IOM list at https://nepal.iom.int/.
5. **Inconsistent academic documents.** Names spelled differently across documents, transcripts not attested, etc.
6. **Credibility interview failure.** Inability to explain your course, why you chose the university, how you'll fund your studies — see file 05.

## Where to apply (biometrics, in Nepal)

VFS Global Kathmandu is the UK Visa Application Centre — https://visa.vfsglobal.com/npl/en/gbr. Book the appointment after submitting the online application and paying fees.
