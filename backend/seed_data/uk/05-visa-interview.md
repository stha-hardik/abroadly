# UK Student Visa Credibility Interview

There is **no compulsory in-person consular interview** for the UK Student visa the way the US has. But UKVI conducts a **Credibility Interview** for a significant share of Nepali applicants — most commonly done by phone or video call after biometrics, sometimes in person at the VAC [Source: GOV.UK Tier 4 / Student visa policy guidance — https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/student-visa-immigration-rules].

If you're invited to interview, you are **not pre-screened for refusal** — UKVI uses it to test whether you're a "genuine student" under the Genuine Student Rule. The interview is conducted in English. A weak interview is one of the top three documented refusal reasons for Nepali applicants.

## When the interview happens

- **Trigger:** UKVI's algorithm flags certain applications — common triggers include applications to lower-ranked institutions, large jumps between previous education and chosen course, gap years, weak financial-funding documentation, or coming from a high-refusal country bucket.
- **Format:** Typically a 15–30 minute phone call from a UK number. Sometimes a video call. Occasionally in person at VFS Kathmandu.
- **Timing:** Usually within 2–3 weeks of biometrics. You won't know in advance — keep your phone on and answer unknown UK numbers (+44).

## What they ask

The questions test three things:

### 1. Why this course at this university

- "Why did you choose [course name]?"
- "Why this specific university and not [closer / cheaper alternative]?"
- "What modules are in your first year?"
- "What's the structure — how many years, how is it assessed?"
- "Who are the professors / module leads, do you know?"
- "What made you apply to UK universities specifically, instead of India / Australia / Nepal?"

Your CAS lists the course name and university — **memorise both**. Read the official course page in detail before the interview. Be ready to name 2-3 modules.

### 2. How you'll pay for it

- "What is the tuition fee for your course?"
- "What are your living costs estimated at?"
- "Who is paying — yourself, parents, a loan?"
- "If parents, what is their occupation and income?"
- "If a loan, which bank, how much, what's the interest rate?"
- "What happens if your funds run out?"

The funds must match what you submitted in your application. If your parents are funding, know their job title and approximate annual income. If a loan, know the bank, amount, and that it is specifically for UK study.

### 3. What you'll do after

- "What are your career plans after the course?"
- "Will you return to Nepal? Why or why not?"
- "What kind of job will this course qualify you for?"
- "Have you researched the job market in your field in Nepal?"

UKVI is checking that you're not using the student visa to work in the UK indefinitely with no intention of completing the course. The honest answer **does not have to be "I will return to Nepal."** Acknowledging that you may use the Graduate Route post-study is fine — but you should be clear that your primary purpose is study.

## Common interview-failure patterns

- **Cannot name modules in your own course.** Suggests you didn't choose the course yourself.
- **Cannot explain your funding numerically.** "My parents will pay" without knowing the tuition figure raises a red flag.
- **Vague or scripted answers.** UKVI interviewers are trained to detect agent-coached responses. Speak naturally, in your own words.
- **English proficiency clearly below your IELTS score.** If your IELTS is 7.0 but you struggle to construct sentences on the call, UKVI will discount the test result.
- **Inconsistent story.** If your SOP says you want a career in software engineering but you tell the interviewer you'll join your family's hotel business, you'll be refused.

## How to prepare

1. **Print and re-read your CAS letter** until you can recite the course title, university name, course duration, tuition fee, and any deposit you've paid.
2. **Print and re-read your Personal Statement / SOP** — every claim there is fair game.
3. **Open the course page** of your university and read the first-year module list out loud.
4. **Practise out loud, not in your head.** Have a friend or family member ask you questions in English; you answer in English.
5. **Know your finances** — exact figures, exact bank, exact source.
6. **Keep your documents handy** — passport, CAS, IELTS report, bank statements. They may ask for a number on the spot.

## If you fail

A refused application can be re-submitted with a new application fee. Address the specific weakness in the refusal letter:

- More detailed personal statement (genuine student credibility).
- Stronger funding documentation (longer balance history, clearer source).
- Better interview preparation.
- Sometimes switching to a less-flagged course / university.

You can also apply for an **Administrative Review** within 28 days of refusal if you believe the decision was a mistake — but this is rare; it costs £80 and is only worth pursuing when there's a clear factual error [Source: GOV.UK Administrative review — https://www.gov.uk/ask-for-a-visa-administrative-review].

## What does NOT happen at the UK interview

- No "DS-160" style two-line consular question. UK interviewers go deep on the three areas above.
- No on-the-spot decision — the interviewer doesn't tell you the outcome. The decision arrives by email/portal.
- No requirement to bring originals to the interview (unless explicitly told).
